She had earned the right to be left in peace.
Byron's fame was, of course, very wonderful, but
it carried with it too many reminders of his
terrible indiscretions - the writing of Don Juan,
which she had never ceased to deplore, his
shocking blasphemies like the Vision of Judgement,
his making friends with the atheist known to
her as 'that infamous Mr. Shelley', and his
mixing with really low and horrid people such
as the subversive journalist Leigh Hunt, whom
on would never conceivably meet in decent society.
