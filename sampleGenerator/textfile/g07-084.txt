'Respecting the will', he wrote to Byron's widow a few days after
hearing its contents, 'the very thought of it is painful to me.
What Mary has said about it is too true.' What Mary, the
new Lady Byron, had said about it was written on the first half
sheet of the same paper: My dearest Annabella, The more we
consider the most prominent subject in your letter, the more
we are convinced of the truth of that dreadful history
connected with it.
