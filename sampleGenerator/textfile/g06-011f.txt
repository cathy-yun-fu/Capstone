By the end of the month he still delighted in Naples. He
told Cloncurry that he enjoyed it as much as his health permitted
him to enjoy anything. 'The Pearl', he wrote, 'is arrived, which is
a great resource. Vesuvius seems to be tired; he is going out
fast.... What a gay, lively people, and what a busy town. At
Rome, every other man was a priest: here the priest is 1superceded
by the soldier - a favourable change in my eye, particularly as
the troops are very fine.'
