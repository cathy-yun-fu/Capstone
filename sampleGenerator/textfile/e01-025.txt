But lace-making is by no means a lost
art. It suffered a decline and fell into
lamentably low standards in the fussy
over-furnishing of the Victorian age
but it would take more than a temporary
lapse in good taste to destroy this lovely,
viable craft. It has, in fact, readily
adapted itself to modern tastes and the ill-
ustrations in the following pages will show
how completely it is in sympathy with con-
temporary surroundings.
