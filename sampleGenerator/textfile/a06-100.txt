Referring to previous negotiations, Mr. Macmillan looked towards
Mr. Reginald Maudling. &quot;These were negotiations in which the
President of the Board of Trade played a conspicuous part,&quot; the
Premier said. He dismissed the idea that Britain would be
swamped by cheap labour. Our industry, he said, would
probably gain. &quot;Many people feel we have had, perhaps, too
much shelter,&quot; he went on.
