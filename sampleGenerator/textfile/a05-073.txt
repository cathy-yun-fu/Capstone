He spent most of his short half-hour on
Berlin. He deplored the possibility of
some &quot;new myth&quot; about betrayal of Germany
by the Allies. &quot;We do not,&quot; he said, &quot;really
know what the Russians want.&quot; But he was
certain that they wanted to establish a
final and irrevocable division of Germany.
Britain could not be a party to an imposed
division. But negotiation, as the debate had
shown, could be undertaken on a variety
of bases.
